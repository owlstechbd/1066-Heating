Having Boiler Bother?

It’s Time to Call in the boiler repair PROs at 1066 Heating

If you’re having problems with your heating system and think the issue is with your boiler, then leave it to 1066 Heating your experts in gas and engineering to sort it out.

Most of the time we just ignore our boilers and trust them to heat our houses and provide us with hot water.

Apart from the occasional check to change the heating temperature gauge and for the annual maintenance check, we hardly even notice they are there.

But as with any piece of essential machinery, we can all experience the occasional problem when things go wrong – we can’t take them completely for granted!

What are the first signs of a Broken boiler?

Usually the first sign that there is a boiler issue, is an unfamiliar or unexpected noise coming from it.

Don’t panic – but you shouldn’t just ignore it.

Even if it still seems to be working and yet is making an unusual noise, you need to get it checked out.

So, check out where the noise is actually coming from…

The cause of the boiler chaos

However, the first sensible thing to do, is to identify where the sound is actually coming from – this will save you time, money and stress.

We all know that our heating and water systems can make odd noises but it’s important to work out whether it really is the boiler or if it’s a noisy radiator or central heating pipe which will probably have different reasons for playing up…

Your boiler – if it’s relatively modern, will have a digital display with various error codes.

When the boiler was installed you were given an instruction manual and so this might be the time to dig it out.

If, like many people, you’ve no idea where your manual is – don’t panic, you can always look online and search for the make and model number of your boiler.

The sounds your boiler could be making may possibly be like a gurgling noise – this means something might need adjusting and a relatively straightforward job such as bleeding the system might be the answer.

This is an easy job and your {Your Company Name} plumbing professional will be able to do it quickly and easily giving you instant peace of mind.

Likewise, the gurgling sound could be that the water pressure is too low.

The pressure will therefore need to be increased until it is restored to its correct level which may involve the topping up of water.

If the noises carry on after the above checks, there’s a possibility it may be the circulation pump that needs attention.

This job will definitely need to be for the {Your Company Name} plumbing pro as it’s likely to be the operating pump working at an excessive speed and will need to be slowed down.

The noise the boiler could be making may be a whooshing sound and the boiler itself could be vibrating which is a scary sight.

Although it’s unlikely there’s imminent danger, it’s a good idea to act and phone {Your Company Name} without delay.

The most common reason your boiler could be unhappy is because of a blockage – this really is the time to call {Your Company Name}!

A whining or buzzing noise being emitted from your boiler is one for concern – it could be caused by worn pump bearings or a defective burner or vibrating fans.
